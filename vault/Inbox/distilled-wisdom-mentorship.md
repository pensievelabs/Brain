## Core Concept: Humans as Distilled Knowledge
High-value individuals—mentors, peers, and close friends—act as repositories of compressed experience. Engaging with them offers a higher information-density-per-minute ratio than consuming static media. They provide "distilled books"—curated, battle-tested wisdom that bypasses the trial-and-error phase of learning.

## Strategy for Retention
*   **Active Curation:** Audit social circle for information density. Prioritize time for those who challenge existing frameworks.
*   **Knowledge Extraction:** Treat conversations as research sessions. Listen for the "how" (the process) rather than just the "what" (the outcome).
*   **Reciprocity:** Maintenance of these connections requires providing equal value. Be a "distilled book" for others.
