## 📒 Summary + Notes

### Medicine 3.0 vs. Medicine 2.0
- **Medicine 2.0**: Reactive. Steps in when something is acutely wrong (infection, broken bone).
- **Medicine 3.0**: Proactive. Tactics must be interwoven into daily life—eating, breathing, and sleeping for longevity.

### The Five Domains of Medicine 3.0
1. **Exercise**: Strength, stability, aerobic efficiency (Zone 2), and peak aerobic capacity (VO2 max). Exercise is the most powerful tool for preventing decline.
2. **Nutrition**: Focus on quality over quantity. Prevent metabolic dysfunction.
3. **Sleep**: Critical for cognitive and physical reset.
4. **Emotional Health**: Largely independent of age; can afflict even the physically healthy.
5. **Exogenous Molecules**: Drugs, hormones, and supplements.

### Key Health Markers
- **Visceral Fat**: Far more dangerous than total body fat. Monitor via annual DEXA scan.
- **Lipoproteins**: Focus on **ApoB** (LDL and others) rather than just "cholesterol." ApoB particles are what actually get stuck in arterial walls.
- **Metabolic Syndrome**: A continuum, not a binary condition. Monitor triglycerides (ideally < 1:1 ratio with HDL), uric acid, and inflammation.
- **Lp(a)**: Test this if there is a family history of premature heart attacks.

### Exercise Specifics
- **Zone 2**: Minimum 3 hours per week (4 sessions of 45 minutes). Stimulates mitochondrial biogenesis.
- **Type 2 Muscle Fibers**: Will wither away without resistance training.
- **Stability**: The foundation for all movement, especially as we age.

### Nutrition & Metabolism
- **Fructose**: Metabolized differently than glucose; triggers uric acid buildup and tricks the body into thinking it's still hungry.
- **Protein**: Crucial for maintaining lean mass. Plant protein is less bioavailable due to fiber.
- **Alcohol/Sugar**: Eliminating or reducing junk food is essential for avoiding diabetes.

### Emotional Health & Reframing
- **Parasuicide**: Slow-rolling into misery via stress, anger, and self-medication.
- **Reframing**: Stepping back from a situation to see what is actually happening rather than reacting reflexively.
