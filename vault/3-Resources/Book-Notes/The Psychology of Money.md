## 🚀 The Book in 3 Sentences
1. Do not judge people's financial decisions without fully understanding their life experiences and the options they have.
2. Astronomical successes and failures are often due to an outsized impact of luck and risk (opportunities, adversities, etc.)
3. Wealth is the option that you have and have not yet exercised. Money can buy you goods or options. Having options gives you freedom.

## 🎨 Impressions
The book talks about a lot of different aspects of wealth and money. Most of the investment advice is on the conservative side (the author calls that out). While this is not an investment book, it broadens our mindset about money, success, and wealth.

#### How I Discovered It
I discovered it from a YouTube video (Ali Abdaal's channel).

#### Who Should Read It?
I think this book is for most people (as everyone deals with money). It helps to build a general thought process about how to not jump to judgments over other people's actions and gives some insights about different topics related to finance, and the history of the modern economy.

## ☘️ How the Book Changed Me
This book gives you a lot to think about. Few of my personal takeaways:
1. Our experience is a very small fraction of the collective experience of humanity. People make financial decisions based on the best knowledge and options available at that time. It makes sense to them.
2. Wealth is the option not exercised right now for a better return in the future.
3. I liked how the author described the formation of bubbles in terms of two different investors and their trading momentum.
