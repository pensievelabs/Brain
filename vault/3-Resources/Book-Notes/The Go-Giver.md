# 🚀 The Book in 3 Sentences
The book is a short story about a struggling go-getter who converts into a successful go-giver businessman through a series of life changing meet ups with other successful go-givers.

It outlines the five main principles of being a pragmatic go-giver and suggests how each one’s success in this sprouts success of another one.

Making a lot of money often means making it via dirty means, the author through this story wants to demonstrate that it does not always have to be this way if we think about the principles in the right way.

# 🎨 Impressions
Overall, I enjoyed reading this book. It is a light read and clarifies your perspective. Often times, we put monetary value as the highest. But it is usually something that follows if you create something that gives value to a lot of people such that they also win in the bargain and you authentically try to do the business.

## Who Should Read It?
This book is a good introduction to the philosophy of good business. Although the story is too simplistic, the principles mentioned are indeed timeless.
Anyone starting their career or looking into starting a new business should read it. Even though you’re someone who is working or already have a business you will certainly benefit from the new perspective.

# ☘️ How the Book Changed Me
There are many useful lessons from this book. The two that resonated with me are the law of value and the law of influence.
Often times we think of a monetary transaction to be strictly that, but sometimes it is just a small value add is what makes a difference. And this is not just true for work or business but also in personal life. People like it when they get a little more and sometimes giving a little more does not have to cost money or resources but it is just a little more care or lending an ear or giving time that makes a difference.
This brings me to the another law that is somewhat connected. If you want to build influence you need genuinely care about helping people. People (including me) think that influence is what makes people listen to you, but it is generally the other way around. Once you start helping people, your influence starts building. Looks for a transaction where it is not 50:50, but each party gets 100% of what they expect to get.

# 📒 Summary + Notes
The five laws of stratospheric success:

**THE LAW OF VALUE**
Your true worth is determined by how much more you give in value than you take in payment.

**THE LAW OF COMPENSATION**
Your income is determined by how many people you serve and how well you serve them.

**THE LAW OF INFLUENCE**
Your influence is determined by how abundantly you place other people’s interests first.

**THE LAW OF AUTHENTICITY**
The most valuable gift you have to offer is yourself.

**THE LAW OF RECEPTIVITY**
The key to effective giving is to stay open to receiving.
